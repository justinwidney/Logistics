OUR ROAD

FROM THEN TO NOW

Edmonton, 1940s. The city was ambitious, poised for prosperity. Morris Byers believed in the potential of Edmonton and choose to purchase a piece of the Future. HE bought a struggling transport company, Blueline Transport, produly renaming it Byers Tranport. The truckline serviced the route between Edmonton and Wanwirght, three days a week. Depsite their best efforts the company began to struggle. Byers turned to a man named Jim Chapman, who owned and operated a terminal on Kingsway avenue. Knowing the Tranportation business the two became partners and a new era began.

Infused with fresh ethusiasm, the company secured financing to enable them to purchase the additonal trucks and trailers necesary to handle their burgeoing business. Growth continued without setback until January, 1951 when Morries Byers died in a accident during a trip to Wabasca. The Chapman family bought the remaining intrestes in the company from the estate, and started expanding into a new terminal in the northeast part of Edmonton.

Dedicated delivery services allow companies to completely outsource their transportation needs, while maintaining benefits as if the client company had its own internal transportation / delivery services.
